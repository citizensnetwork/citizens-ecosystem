> This file is not documentation. It is our **north star and conscience**.
> Read it before you plan, before you build, and again before you ship.
> Its job is to make sure every line of code — however small — moves us
> toward what Citizens exists to do. If an action doesn't, we change the

## Language

"Kingdom" - The entire inclusive Christian ecosystem, including people,
  organisations, initiatives, principles, beliefs and activity.
"Body" - Another reference to the Christian community, with an additional emphasis
  on the collaborative nature between multiple functional pieces
"Citizens" - Every Christian person, whether part of an organisation or not;
  every Christian believer is first a Citizen and child in God's Kingdom.
"Contributors" - Christian and faith-rooted organisations, whether companies,
  non-profits, marketplaces or any formal helps. Reference also includes people
  who are actively helping, serving, adding or to Christian activity.

## Why we exist

Citizens exists to **Connect citizens to their ideal spaces, contributors to their
ideal members, and needs to their ideal heroes. In doing so, stimulating an
interconnected lifestyle and ecosystem of Christian activity - Using connection,
visibility and collaboration as means to holistically serve the needs of citizens,
contributors and social needs**

We highlight and support every Christian in the city by making
initiatives and citizens alike seen, known, served, connectable,
included and recognized as a crucial parts of the Christian
community - De-scattering the body of Christ and making
cross-sector-collaboration a norm; Boosting real Kingdom lifestyle.

We are unapologetically a Christian community platform. The language,
categories, visual identity, and tone reflect that without apology — welcoming
and modelling the rich and strong Kingdom culture to all who stumble upon it.

## What Citizens is — the intersection

Citizens is a **genuine intersection between citizens, organisations and societal needs**, built
to connect and uplift **All three at once**:

- **For the citizen** — Helping each member find their place in the body - discovering
  belonging in the extreme versatility of Christian activity in the city - communities,
  organisations, ministries, events, fun and help they never knew existed that serve,
  empower, support or connect them and/or their family in whatever capacity they need.
- **For the organisation (contributor)** — being highlighted, supported, and exposed
  to ideal candidates across the nation, integrating features that inspire community input,
  service and participation - making connection fun and easy for anyone who find them.
- **For social needs** — Providing an environment where each each role player is
  provided opportunities to serve at events or organisations, collaborate in community work,
  post community improvement ideas for public participation and form part of socially uplifting
  initiatives.

This is **simultaneous Kingdom discovery** And beyond discovery,
we connect every player to **real needs in society** — enabling collaboration
citizen↔citizen, citizen↔organisation, and organisation↔organisation — for
shared, tangible impact.

> Every citizen and every contributor — **no matter how small** — is a
> contributing, important, and necessary part of the whole Body. Citizens is
> the means by which they host and find their place in it.

## The highest aspiration

A city — then cities — where division in the Christian community decreases,
where collaboration across sectors is normal, where no organisation or citizen
doing good work stays invisible, and where any believer looking to serve can
immediately find a way to act. Citizens Connect is the platform where Pretoria's
Kingdom community makes itself visible; the ecosystem is the infrastructure for that
across every dimension of community life.

## The ecosystem (one north star, many channels)

- **Citizens Connect** — the discovery + connection layer (current focus). Map,
  events, places, contributors, community.
- **Citizens Vision** — the intelligence/impact layer: organisations see their
  real community impact, reach, and engagement.
- **Citizens Wear** — Kingdom-aligned fashion and ethical, community-rooted brands.
- **Citizens Impact** — where community change is proposed, agreed, funded and
  officially launched; uniting large financial role players and organisations in
  addressing community needs.
- **Citizens Equip** — Discovery of Christian educational, equipping and training organisations
- **Citizens Play** — Cloud based Christian platform providing storage, resources, tools
  games and entertainment to break the ice, share fun and share memories for Chrsitian gatherings of any size.

## THE ALIGNMENT SELF-PROMPT (run this every implementation)

Before starting, and again before shipping, ask of the work in front of you:

**1. Mutual discovery —** Does this help a citizen discover something (an org,
event, ministry, help) they didn't know existed, *and* help that org be found by
those who need it? If it only serves one side, how can it serve both?

**2. Connection to real need —** Does it connect a person or organisation to an
actual need in society, and make collaboration (cit↔cit, cit↔org, org↔org)
easier — not just add noise or a feature for its own sake?

**3. The small are honoured —** Does it treat the smallest contributor and the
quietest citizen as necessary parts of the Body? No feature should privilege the
big and bury the small. Does it lift the unseen?

**4. Kingdom-clear —** Is the purpose and framing true to "Connecting the
Kingdom"? Does it deepen unity rather than division? Would it make sense to the
believer looking to serve and to the seeker just arriving?

**5. Excellence as stewardship —** Is it A+ quality, light, fast, secure, and
built to scale? Excellence here is worship and good stewardship of the people,
time, and trust this platform carries.

**5. Intuitive Service —** Is there any better way this could better benefit and
aid the user, whether citizen, admin or organisation?

### Then grade it honestly

- **Does the code currently reflect this vision?** Where it does, name it. Where
  it doesn't, say so plainly and note the gap.
- **Does what we are working on right now build toward this?** If yes, how. If
  not, should it be reshaped, deprioritised, or dropped?
- **What can we keep doing to get closer?** One concrete next step.
- **How can this very action advance the vision even to the smallest degree?**
  There is almost always a 1% move — a clearer label, a surfaced unknown org, a
  faster path for someone to serve. Take it.

> If you cannot connect the current task to at least one litmus test above,
> pause and ask before continuing. Building the wrong thing excellently is still
> the wrong thing.
